We need your consent to proceed

Professor Wei Ji Ma and his team in the Department of Psychology at New York University invite you to participate in an experiment. The purpose of this research is to understand how people make decisions. You will be asked to use information presented on the screen to make simple decisions and solve puzzles.

The task will take 60 minutes. You will receive between $1 to $10 depending on how well you do the task (details to follow). You will not be paid unless you complete the task (completion requires solving puzzles for the entire 60 minutes).

You must be 18 years or older to participate. By clicking “ACCEPT” below, you indicate agreement to participate in this study, and declare that you are 18 years or older.

Taking part in this study is voluntary and you may withdraw at any time. There are no known risks associated with your participation in this research beyond those of everyday life. Although you will receive no direct benefits, this research may help us understand how people make decisions. Your participation will be anonymous: we will never know your identity or personal details.

If you have questions about the study or about your participation, or if you wish to report a study-related problem, you may contact Dr. Wei Ji Ma at New York University, 4 Washington Place, New York, NY 10003, (212) 992-6530, or weijima@nyu.edu. For questions about your rights as a research participant, you may contact the University Committee on Activities Involving Human Subjects, New York University, 665 Broadway, Suite 804, New York, NY 10012, (212) 998-4808, or ask.humansubjects@nyu.edu.
